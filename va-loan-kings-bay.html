Why VA Loans Are a Strong Choice Near Kings Bay

  • 0% Down Payment: Purchase with no money down — no need to save tens of thousands before buying
  • No Monthly Mortgage Insurance: Unlike FHA or conventional loans under 20% down, VA loans have no PMI
  • Competitive Rates: VA loan rates are often lower than conventional financing
  • Flexible Credit: The VA does not set a hard minimum credit score — I work with all credit levels
  • Seller-Paid Closing Costs: In Camden County's market, asking sellers for 3-6% toward closing costs is common
  • USDA Alternative in Woodbine: Much of Camden County qualifies for USDA loans with 0% down — a great option if you want to preserve VA entitlement

Using Your BAH Near Kings Bay

Kings Bay BAH rates are designed to cover median housing costs in Camden County. When combined with a VA loan's no-down-payment benefit, many service members find their BAH covers most or all of their monthly mortgage payment.

The exact fit depends on your rank, dependency status, and the home you purchase — but it's worth running the numbers before assuming you need to rent.

How Much Can You Buy — and How Much Entitlement Do You Have?

In 2026, there is no VA loan limit for borrowers with full entitlement — you can purchase a home at any price point with 0% down, subject to lender approval. If you have partial entitlement (you have an active VA loan elsewhere), different limits may apply.

Second-Tier Entitlement at Kings Bay

Many submariners receive orders to Kings Bay more than once. Second-tier entitlement may allow you to purchase a new home at Kings Bay while keeping a previous VA-financed home — without having to sell first. This is a conversation worth having before your orders drop.

Buying vs. Renting Near Kings Bay

With no down payment required and BAH rates covering much of the mortgage payment, buying often makes financial sense even for a 2-3 year tour at Kings Bay. Rental inventory near the base is limited, which means prices are rising — and your BAH goes to someone else's equity instead of building your own.

The VA Loan Home Buying Process — Step by Step

  1. Get Pre-Approved — Ideally before orders drop. Pre-approval strengthens your offer and tells you exactly what you can buy.
  2. Certificate of Eligibility (COE) — I pull this for you as part of the pre-approval process.
  3. Find a VA-Friendly Realtor — In Camden County, work with an agent experienced in VA transactions and VA appraisal requirements.
  4. Make an Offer — With pre-approval in hand, you can act fast in a competitive market.
  5. VA Appraisal & Inspection — The VA requires an appraisal to confirm the home meets Minimum Property Requirements (MPR). A separate home inspection is also strongly recommended.
  6. Clear to Close — Typical timeline is 30-45 days from ratified contract.
  7. Close & Move In — Remote closings are available if you're PCSing from another duty station.

Pre-Qualification vs. Pre-Approval — Know the Difference

Not all pre-approvals are equal. A pre-qualification is an estimate based on self-reported information. A pre-approval involves verified income, assets, and credit — and carries significantly more weight with sellers in a competitive market like Camden County.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use a VA loan near Kings Bay with no down payment?

Yes. The VA loan benefit allows eligible service members, veterans, and surviving spouses to purchase with 0% down and no PMI. Rates are typically competitive with or better than conventional financing.

Can my BAH cover my mortgage payment?

In many cases, yes. Kings Bay BAH rates are designed to reflect median housing costs in Camden County. When combined with no down payment, many service members find BAH covers most or all of their monthly payment. See the full breakdown →

Can I use my VA loan more than once?

Yes. Many submariners use their VA benefit multiple times. Second-tier entitlement may allow you to purchase at Kings Bay while keeping a previous VA-financed home. Learn how entitlement works →

How long does a VA loan take to close?

Typically 30-45 days from ratified contract. Getting pre-approved before orders drop speeds the process considerably and strengthens your offer.

What credit score do I need?

The VA does not set a hard minimum. Most lenders look for 580-620+. I work with all credit levels and can help map out a path to qualification.

Can I close remotely if I'm PCSing from another duty station?

Yes. Remote closings are available. I specialize in helping military families coordinate closings around PCS timelines and command schedules.
