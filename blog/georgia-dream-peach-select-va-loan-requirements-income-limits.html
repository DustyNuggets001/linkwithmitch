Georgia Dream Peach Select VA Loan Program: Requirements, Income Limits, and How It Works

The Georgia Dream Peach Select VA loan program offers eligible homebuyers a 30-year fixed VA mortgage with below-market rates, no down payment requirement, and flexible qualification guidelines.

For eligible homebuyers in Georgia, the Peach Select VA loan program can provide lower interest rates and 100% financing—but there are important income limits, credit requirements, and borrower contribution rules to understand before applying.

Georgia offers several programs designed to make homeownership more affordable, and one of the most attractive options for eligible buyers is the Peach Select VA loan through the Georgia Dream program. This option pairs the benefits of a traditional VA loan with state-backed financing that can offer below-market interest rates.

For many military families and veterans living in Coastal Georgia—including those stationed at Kings Bay Naval Submarine Base or relocating to Camden County—this program can provide a competitive path to buying a home in communities like Kingsland, St. Marys, or Woodbine. However, the Peach Select option has specific qualification rules, income limits, and borrower contribution requirements that are important to understand before applying.

What the Georgia Dream Peach Select VA Loan Program Is

The Georgia Dream Peach Select VA loan program is a specialized mortgage option offered through the Georgia Dream Homeownership Program. It is designed for borrowers who qualify for VA financing and meet the state program's income and credit guidelines. Unlike standard VA loans offered by most lenders, the Peach Select option is paired with state funding that may provide lower interest rates than typical market VA loans.

For buyers in Coastal Georgia, this can be especially appealing when purchasing homes in areas such as Camden County or near Kings Bay Naval Submarine Base. Military families frequently relocating through PCS orders may find the reduced interest rate helpful for managing long-term housing costs.

  • VA-backed financing: The loan uses the standard VA loan benefit, meaning eligible borrowers can still purchase a home with no down payment.
  • 30-year fixed-rate mortgage: The program offers a fixed interest rate for the life of the loan, providing stable monthly payments.
  • Below-market interest rates: Because the program is funded through Georgia Dream, interest rates may be lower than traditional VA loan options depending on market conditions.

The program works best for buyers who qualify under the program's income limits and who want a simple fixed-rate mortgage without layering multiple assistance programs.

Who Qualifies for the Peach Select VA Program

To qualify for the Peach Select VA loan program, borrowers must first meet the eligibility requirements for a standard VA loan. This means the borrower must be an eligible veteran, active-duty service member, or qualifying military spouse with a valid Certificate of Eligibility.

Many buyers stationed at Kings Bay Naval Submarine Base qualify through active-duty service, while veterans who previously served in the area may also be eligible. Because Coastal Georgia communities like Kingsland and St. Marys have a strong military presence, the program is often used by both current service members and recently separated veterans.

General program eligibility

In addition to VA eligibility, the Georgia Dream Peach Select program includes several state-level requirements that borrowers must meet.

Borrowers must fall within program income limits based on household size and county location. The property must also meet purchase price limits established by the Georgia Dream program. These limits are designed to ensure the program supports primary residence homebuyers rather than high-value investment properties.

The property must be used as a primary residence, which aligns well with military families moving into the area through a permanent change of station. Buyers relocating through a PCS move can often purchase a home soon after arriving, especially if they have already used their VA benefit before. If you're unsure how repeat VA use works, this guide on using VA loan benefit multiple times explains how eligibility can be reused.

Credit Score Requirements, Income Limits, and Borrower Contribution

The Peach Select VA loan program has several qualification requirements that differ slightly from standard VA loan guidelines. While the VA itself does not set a minimum credit score, the Georgia Dream program establishes its own credit threshold for borrowers.

Typically, borrowers need a minimum credit score of around 640 to qualify for the Peach Select option. This requirement helps ensure the program maintains lower interest rates and manageable risk levels for the state-backed financing structure.

Income limits and borrower contribution

The program also includes household income limits. These limits vary based on county and household size, and they apply to all income earned by adults living in the home—not just the borrowers on the loan. For buyers in Camden County or other parts of Coastal Georgia, the limits are designed to support moderate-income households.

Unlike some other Georgia Dream options, the Peach Select VA program does not include down payment assistance. However, it still allows up to 100% financing through the VA loan benefit, meaning no down payment is required.

The program does require a small borrower contribution toward the transaction. This minimum contribution is typically $500, which must come from the borrower's own funds. The contribution demonstrates borrower investment in the purchase while still keeping the overall upfront cost relatively low.

For military families relocating to Kings Bay through a PCS move, understanding these guidelines early can help simplify the homebuying timeline. Buyers planning a move can learn more about financing strategies during relocation in this guide to VA loans during a PCS move.

When a Standard VA Loan May Be Better Than the Georgia Dream Peach Select Program

The Georgia Dream Peach Select VA loan program is often one of the most competitive mortgage options available for eligible buyers because of its below-market interest rates. However, there are situations where a standard VA loan may still be the better choice.

The biggest limitation of the Peach Select program is the household income cap. Because the program is designed to help moderate-income homebuyers, households earning above the program limits cannot qualify even if they meet VA loan eligibility requirements. In those cases, a traditional VA loan through a standard lender may provide more flexibility.

Another scenario involves buyers who need more flexibility with property type, timing, or financing structure. Standard VA loans may allow more options for certain loan structures, lender overlays, or property situations that fall outside Georgia Dream guidelines.

For many military families moving to Coastal Georgia, especially those relocating through a PCS move, comparing both options early in the process can help identify the best path. The right choice depends on income, credit, and overall financial goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

What credit score is required for the Peach Select VA loan program?

The Georgia Dream Peach Select VA program typically requires a minimum credit score of about 640. This requirement is set by the state program rather than the VA itself, which technically does not have a minimum score requirement.

Does the Peach Select program allow 100% financing with no down payment?

Yes. Because the program uses the VA loan benefit, eligible borrowers can still purchase a home with no down payment. However, borrowers must provide a small minimum contribution toward the transaction.

Why doesn't the Peach Select VA program include down payment assistance?

The Peach Select program focuses on providing lower interest rates rather than combining multiple assistance programs. Because VA loans already allow 100% financing, the program instead emphasizes reduced interest costs over time.

What are the income limits for Georgia Dream Peach Select loans?

Income limits vary depending on county and household size. The program considers total household income for all adults living in the home, not just the borrowers on the loan. Limits are designed to support moderate-income buyers purchasing a primary residence.

Key Takeaways

  • The Georgia Dream Peach Select VA loan program offers a 30-year fixed VA mortgage with potentially below-market interest rates.
  • Eligible borrowers can still purchase a home with 100% financing using their VA loan benefit.
  • The program requires a minimum credit score around 640 and applies household income limits.
  • Borrowers must provide a minimum $500 contribution toward the transaction.
  • The program may be especially helpful for military families and veterans buying homes in Coastal Georgia communities near Kings Bay.
