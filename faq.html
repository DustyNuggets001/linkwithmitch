Frequently Asked Questions

Get Answers About VA Loans, PCS Moves, Down Payment Assistance & More

Do you offer down payment assistance or first-time buyer programs?

+

Yes. I help first-time and repeat buyers explore a wide range of down payment assistance (DPA) and low- or no-down-payment programs. This includes statewide options like Georgia Dream, national programs through FHLBank (FHLB), and additional resources available through our lender partnerships.

For eligible buyers in Camden County and surrounding areas, many locations also qualify for USDA financing, which can offer a 0% down payment option depending on income limits and property location. I review your goals and eligibility to help match you with the programs or loan structures that best fit your situation.

Can I have two VA loans at the same time?

+

In some situations, yes. Some service members and veterans can use remaining VA entitlement to purchase a new home while keeping their current VA-financed property. This is common during PCS moves when selling the previous home isn't practical or desirable.

Whether you qualify depends on your available entitlement, the size of the new loan, and local county limits. I help buyers calculate their remaining entitlement, understand second-tier VA guidelines, and decide whether carrying two VA loans makes sense for their goals.

Can I use my VA home loan benefit more than once?

+

Yes. Many service members and veterans can use their VA loan benefit more than once. Your entitlement may be fully restored after selling a home with a VA loan and paying off the loan in full, and in some cases you may even be able to use remaining entitlement to purchase again without selling your previous property.

I help buyers understand how VA entitlement works, how restoration applies, and how to plan future purchases as duty stations or life circumstances change. If you're unsure about your current entitlement or eligibility, I can walk you through it step by step.

Do you assist with VA IRRRL (streamlined) refinances?

+

Yes. I have extensive experience helping service members and veterans use the VA IRRRL (Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan), the VA's streamlined refinance option for existing VA mortgages. An IRRRL can simplify the process of updating your current VA loan by reducing documentation, speeding up timelines, and making the refinance more straightforward than a traditional refinance.

I explain what to expect, review your goals, and walk you through the steps so you can make a well-informed decision. I can help you determine whether an IRRRL may fit your situation.

Can I rent out my home if I bought it with a VA loan?

+

VA loans are intended for primary residences, so at the time you purchase with a VA loan, you must certify that you intend to occupy the property as your primary home. However, life and duty stations can change.

After you have met the occupancy requirement, many borrowers do later move and convert a VA-financed property to a rental. Whether this is appropriate depends on your situation, future plans, and current VA guidelines. I can't provide legal or tax advice, but I can help explain how the VA side typically looks and recommend that you consult with appropriate professionals before making a decision.
