Second-Tier Entitlement Options

Many service members don't realize you may be able to own two VA-financed homes simultaneously in certain situations.

How It May Work

If you've already used part of your VA loan entitlement on your current home, you may have remaining entitlement available for a second VA loan, depending on your specific situation.

Potential PCS Scenarios

  • You bought a home at your first duty station with a VA loan and get PCS orders to a new base‚Äîyou may be able to keep your current home as a rental while purchasing at your new location
  • You're returning to a previous duty station where you already own a VA-financed home
  • You want to explore keeping your current home while buying in a new location

Why Consider Keeping Your Home

  • Build long-term wealth through real estate
  • Rental income may offset mortgage costs
  • You may have a home ready if you return to that duty station
  • Potential long-term financial benefits

Important: Second-tier entitlement is complex and depends on many factors including your current loan balance, remaining entitlement, purchase price at your new location, and county loan limits. Let's discuss your specific situation to determine if this option is available to you.

Who Qualifies for VA Loans?

Active Duty

After 90 continuous days of active service (181 days during peacetime)

Veterans

Honorable discharge after meeting minimum service requirements

National Guard & Reserves

After 6 years of service (or 90 days active duty under Title 10 or Title 32)

Surviving Spouses

Spouses of service members who died in service or from service-connected disabilities

Need your Certificate of Eligibility (COE)? I can help you obtain it quickly through the VA's online system or submit Form 26-1880.

  • Contact me for pre-qualification
  • I'll help you obtain your VA Certificate of Eligibility (COE)
  • Review BAH for your new duty station
  • Research neighborhoods and schools

60-90 Days Before PCS

  • Finalize pre-qualification
  • Start virtual home tours if possible
  • Identify target neighborhoods
  • Connect with local real estate agent

30-60 Days Before PCS

  • Finalize pre-approval
  • House hunting (virtual or in-person)

  • Begin loan processing
  • Schedule home inspection
