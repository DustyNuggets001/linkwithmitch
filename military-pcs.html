Understanding Second-Tier Entitlement

Many service members don't realize you can potentially own two VA-financed homes simultaneously using second-tier entitlement.

How It Works

Your full VA loan entitlement is currently $766,550 in most counties (higher in some high-cost areas). If you've already used part of your entitlement on your current home, you may have remaining entitlement available for a second VA loan.

Common PCS Scenarios

  • Scenario 1: You bought a home at your first duty station with a VA loan. You get PCS orders to a new base. Instead of selling, you can rent out your current home and use second-tier entitlement to buy at your new location.
  • Scenario 2: You're a submariner at Kings Bay who previously bought there, got orders elsewhere, and now you're coming back. You can keep your rental and buy another home at Kings Bay.
  • Scenario 3: You own a VA-financed home worth $250K (loan balance $220K). You have $546,550 of remaining entitlement available for a second purchase.

Benefits of Keeping Your Home as a Rental

  • Build long-term wealth through real estate appreciation
  • Rental income may cover your mortgage payment
  • You already have a home if you get orders back to that duty station
  • Potential tax benefits (consult your tax advisor)

Important: Second-tier entitlement requires careful planning and calculations. I'll analyze your current loan balance, remaining entitlement, and purchase price at your new location to determine if you qualify.

Who Qualifies for VA Loans?

Active Duty

After 90 continuous days of active service (181 days during peacetime)

Veterans

Honorable discharge after meeting minimum service requirements

National Guard & Reserves

After 6 years of service (or 90 days active duty under Title 10 or Title 32)

Surviving Spouses

Spouses of service members who died in service or from service-connected disabilities
