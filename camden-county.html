Why Choose Camden County?

Camden County offers coastal living, military community support, outdoor recreation, and small-town charmâ€”all within reach of Georgia's Golden Isles and North Florida.

Waterfront & Outdoor Recreation

Camden County is bordered by the Atlantic Ocean, the St. Marys River, and the Intracoastal Waterway. Residents enjoy boating, fishing, kayaking, and access to pristine natural areas including:

  • Crooked River State Park - Camping, hiking trails, kayaking, and waterfront picnic areas
  • Cumberland Island National Seashore - Pristine beaches, wild horses, and historic sites accessible by ferry from St. Marys
  • St. Marys Waterfront - Riverfront parks, restaurants, shops, and community events
  • Satilla River - Paddling, fishing, and wildlife viewing in one of Georgia's most scenic blackwater rivers

Military Community & Kings Bay

Naval Submarine Base Kings Bay is the East Coast home of the Ohio-class ballistic-missile and guided-missile submarines. The base is a cornerstone of Camden County's economy and community, bringing thousands of military families to the area.

Camden County offers strong support for military families through local organizations, veteran services, and community programs. Many neighborhoods and schools are military-friendly, and the area is well-versed in the unique needs of PCS relocations and VA home loans.

Small Towns with Coastal Access

Camden County's communities each offer their own character:

  • Kingsland - The county's largest city, with shopping, dining, and easy access to I-95
  • St. Marys - Historic downtown waterfront with riverfront parks, local shops, and the gateway to Cumberland Island
  • Woodbine - The county seat with a quieter, rural atmosphere and historic charm

Residents enjoy affordable living, low property taxes compared to nearby metro areas, and proximity to Jacksonville, FL (45 minutes) and Brunswick, GA (30 minutes) for additional shopping, dining, and entertainment.

Year-Round Mild Climate

Camden County enjoys a temperate coastal climate with mild winters and warm summers, making outdoor activities enjoyable year-round. The area's proximity to the ocean provides cooling breezes and access to beaches on nearby barrier islands.

Local Points of Interest

Cumberland Island National Seashore

Georgia's largest and southernmost barrier island. Accessible only by ferry or private boat, Cumberland Island offers 18 miles of pristine beaches, maritime forests, and the historic Dungeness ruins.

Crooked River State Park

A beautiful state park offering camping, cabin rentals, hiking trails, a boat ramp, and access to the tidal marshes and rivers that define coastal Georgia.

St. Marys Historic District

Walk the charming streets of Georgia's second-oldest city. Enjoy waterfront dining, unique shops, Oak Grove Cemetery, and the annual Rock Shrimp Festival.

Satilla River

One of the most scenic blackwater rivers in Georgia, perfect for kayaking, canoeing, fishing, and wildlife photography. Launch sites available throughout the county.

Orange Hall House Museum

A historic Greek Revival mansion in St. Marys showcasing 19th-century architecture and the history of Camden County's early settlers.

Proximity to Jekyll Island & St. Simons Island

Just 30-40 minutes north, Georgia's Golden Isles offer world-class beaches, golf courses, dining, and resort amenitiesâ€”perfect for day trips or weekend getaways.
